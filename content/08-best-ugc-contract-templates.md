## What to Look For in a UGC Contract Template

Whatever you choose, make sure it includes:

### Must-Have Clauses

1. **Detailed scope of work** ‚Äî Number of deliverables, formats, platforms
2. **Revision limits** ‚Äî Don't agree to unlimited revisions
3. **Payment terms** ‚Äî Amount, timeline, late fees
4. **Usage rights** ‚Äî Organic, paid, duration, exclusivity
5. **Kill fee** ‚Äî Compensation if they cancel mid-project
6. **Content ownership** ‚Äî Who owns what, portfolio rights

### Nice-to-Have Features

- Built-in e-signature (saves time and looks professional)
- Auto-fill fields (your name, rate, etc.)
- Multiple template types (different contract for different deal types)
- PDF export
- Mobile-friendly signing

## How to Actually Use Your Template

Having a template is step one. Here's how to use it effectively:

1. **Customize once** ‚Äî Fill in your standard info (name, payment details, base terms)
2. **Adjust per deal** ‚Äî Update scope, pricing, and usage rights for each project
3. **Send before starting work** ‚Äî No signed contract = no content
4. **Keep records** ‚Äî Save all signed contracts in a folder organized by client
5. **Follow up** ‚Äî If they don't sign within 48 hours, nudge them
